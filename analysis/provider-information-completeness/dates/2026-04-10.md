## Observations

- The local provider model has a clear split between summary fields and enriched detail fields, but most meaningful differentiators only appear after detail enrichment.
- The runtime already enriches search results before recommending them, so the agent usually sees richer data than the initial search payload.
- In the live sample of 41 providers across 6 categories:
  - summary completeness was 0 for promo, description, services, and terms fields;
  - detail completeness reached 63.4% for structured promo, 68.3% for service highlights, 70.7% for terms highlights, and 100% for description snippets;
  - rating was technically present for all providers, but 39/41 values were `0.0`;
  - 36/41 providers had no structured price anchor and 24/41 had no location;
  - `eventTypes` was empty in all sampled detail payloads.
- The full marketplace census confirmed the same shape at population scale:
  - 180 providers were reported by the marketplace endpoint and all 180 detail records were fetched successfully;
  - summary completeness remained 0 for promo, description, services, and terms fields;
  - detail completeness was 40.6% for structured promo, 44.4% for service highlights, 44.4% for terms highlights, and 100% for description snippets;
  - 176/180 ratings were `0.0`, 146/180 providers had no structured price anchor, 121/180 had no location, and 180/180 had empty `eventTypes`;
  - 97 providers signaled promo-like offers in the title while leaving `promoBadge` and `promoSummary` null.
- Full-census differentiation was strong after detail enrichment in most categories, but not perfect:
  - 15 of 16 categories had fully unique detail signatures;
  - `Bebés` still had residual collisions, with 35 providers compressing to 32 detail signatures.
- Detail-level differentiator signatures were unique within every sampled category, but several distinctions were driven by long prose snippets or embedded promo text rather than reliable structured fields.
- Some extraction heuristics pull noisy lines into `serviceHighlights` or `termsHighlights`, for example `Preguntar por paquetes` or `Consultar términos y condiciones`.
- Promo information is inconsistent: several providers expose discounts in the title or description while leaving `promoBadge` and `promoSummary` null.

## Findings Promoted

- Promoted the conclusion that summary data is too weak for differentiation without detail enrichment.
- Promoted the stronger conclusion that detail data is generally enough for textual differentiation across the full marketplace, but still weak for structured comparison.
- Promoted the recommendation to improve upstream structured fields for price, location, event types, promos, and service taxonomy.
